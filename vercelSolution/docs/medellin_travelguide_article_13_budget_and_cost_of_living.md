# Budget and cost of living in Medellín

Medellín is one of the best value-for-money cities in Latin America. Here's roughly what things cost in 2026, in **Colombian Pesos (COP)** and US dollars (assuming ~4,000 COP = 1 USD).

## Daily budgets (per person)

- **Backpacker**: 120,000 – 200,000 COP (~USD 30–50) per day
  - Hostel dorm, street food + cheap menús del día, public transit.
- **Mid-range traveler**: 250,000 – 500,000 COP (~USD 60–125) per day
  - Private Airbnb or boutique hotel, mix of cafés and nicer restaurants, occasional tours.
- **Comfort / digital nomad**: 600,000 – 1,200,000 COP (~USD 150–300) per day
  - Nice furnished apartment in Poblado, every meal out, daily Ubers, weekly tours.

## Typical prices

- **Tinto (small coffee)**: 3,000–5,000 COP
- **Specialty pour-over**: 12,000–18,000 COP
- **Menú del día (3-course lunch)**: 15,000–25,000 COP
- **Sit-down dinner at a nice restaurant**: 50,000–120,000 COP
- **Local beer (Aguila / Poker / Club Colombia)**: 4,000–8,000 COP at a tienda, 12,000–18,000 in a bar
- **Cocktail at a Provenza bar**: 30,000–45,000 COP
- **Uber across town (10 km)**: 15,000–25,000 COP
- **Metro ride**: ~3,300 COP
- **Massage (1 hour)**: 80,000–150,000 COP
- **Gym day pass**: 20,000–40,000 COP
- **Furnished 1-bedroom Airbnb in Poblado (monthly)**: 2,500,000 – 5,000,000 COP (~USD 625–1,250)

## Money tips

- **Carry small bills.** Many places don't have change for 50,000 or 100,000 notes.
- **Use cards everywhere except small tiendas and street vendors.**
- **ATMs**: Bancolombia and Davivienda are reliable. Withdraw inside a bank or supermarket.
- **Tipping**: 10% is often added to the bill (*propina sugerida*). It's optional but customary to leave it.
- **Bargain politely** at markets and with private drivers, never in restaurants or shops with marked prices.
