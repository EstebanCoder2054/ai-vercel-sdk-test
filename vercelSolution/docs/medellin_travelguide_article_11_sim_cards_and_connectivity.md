# SIM cards, eSIMs, and staying connected in Medellín

Mobile data in Colombia is cheap and easy. You have three options, depending on how long you're staying and how comfortable you are with paperwork.

## Option 1: eSIM (easiest)

Best for trips under 30 days or if you don't want to deal with a physical SIM.

- **Airalo / Holafly / Nomad** — eSIMs you can install before you even land. Around USD 8–25 for 1–30 days of data.
- Trade-off: usually data-only (no local phone number).

## Option 2: Local prepaid SIM

Cheaper for longer stays. You'll need your **passport** to register.

- **Claro** — the largest network, best coverage outside the city.
- **Movistar** — solid coverage, often cheaper plans.
- **Tigo / WOM** — competitive, sometimes better promos.

Where to buy:
- Inside any major shopping mall (Santafé, El Tesoro, Oviedo).
- Cell phone shops along Carrera 70 in Laureles.

A typical 30-day plan costs **30,000–50,000 COP** for unlimited social apps + 20–40 GB of data.

## Option 3: Roaming from your home carrier

Usually the most expensive. Some US plans (T-Mobile, Google Fi) include Colombia for free — worth checking before you go.

## Wi-Fi

- **Cafés, hotels, malls** all have free Wi-Fi. Speeds are good in the city.
- **Coworking spaces** (see [[medellin_travelguide_article_14_coworking_and_remote_work]]) have fiber connections.

## Useful apps to install before arriving

- **Uber / DiDi / Cabify** — ride-hailing
- **Rappi** — Colombia's super-app for food, groceries, pharmacy
- **WhatsApp** — everyone uses it. Restaurants, tours, even your barber.
- **Civica** (if staying long) — top up the metro card from your phone
- **Google Translate** — download Spanish for offline use
