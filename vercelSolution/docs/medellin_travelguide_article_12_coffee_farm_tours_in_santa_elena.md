# Coffee farm tours in Santa Elena and the surrounding hills

You don't have to drive 5 hours to the Eje Cafetero to visit a real coffee farm. Several working *fincas* sit in the green hills surrounding Medellín — most within an hour of El Poblado.

## What a typical tour includes

- A walk through the coffee plantation with the farmer (or a guide working with them)
- Picking ripe cherries off the plant by hand
- Watching (or doing) the depulping, fermenting, washing, and drying steps
- Roasting a small batch of beans
- A cupping — tasting several profiles brewed fresh
- A traditional *paisa* lunch on the farm

Most tours run **3 to 5 hours** and cost **80,000 – 200,000 COP** per person.

## Recommended farms

- **Finca La Mesa** (San Cristóbal, ~45 min west)
  - Family-run, intimate groups, English-speaking host.

- **Finca El Cielo** (Santa Elena, ~1 hour east)
  - Beautiful views over Medellín, great for photography.

- **La Loma Coffee Farm** (San Antonio de Prado)
  - One of the closest options, easy half-day trip.

## What to wear

- Long pants and closed shoes — the plants are dense and you'll be on dirt paths.
- A light rain jacket — these hills get more rain than the city.
- A hat and sunscreen.

## Pro tips

- **Book in advance.** Most farms only take small groups (4–10 people) and fill up on weekends.
- **Combine with Santa Elena's flower farms** if you go east. The *silleteros* who march in the Feria de las Flores live up there.
- **Buy beans direct from the farm** — fresher than anything you'll find in a city café, and you're paying the farmer directly.

If you want a longer experience, the real coffee region (Salento, Filandia, Jardín) is 4–6 hours away and worth a multi-day side trip — see [[medellin_travelguide_article_15_side_trips_beyond_medellin]].
