# Spanish phrases for travelers in Medellín

Colombian Spanish — especially the *paisa* accent of Medellín — is considered one of the clearest in Latin America. Locals appreciate any attempt to speak it, even badly. Here's a starter kit.

## The essentials

- **Hola / Buenos días / Buenas tardes / Buenas noches** — Hi / Good morning / Good afternoon / Good evening
- **Por favor / Gracias / De nada** — Please / Thank you / You're welcome
- **¿Cuánto cuesta?** — How much does it cost?
- **La cuenta, por favor** — The check, please
- **No entiendo** — I don't understand
- **¿Habla inglés?** — Do you speak English?

## Paisa slang you'll actually hear

- **¡Qué chimba!** — How cool! (very common, mildly informal)
- **Parcero / Parce** — Buddy, friend (the most paisa word of all)
- **¡Hágale pues!** — Go for it / OK then / Let's do it
- **Bacano / Chévere** — Cool, nice
- **¿Qué más?** — What's up? (literally "what else?")
- **¡Hijuepucha!** — A mild exclamation, like "dang!" (the unprintable version replaces *pucha* with something stronger)
- **Mi amor / mi vida / mi reina** — Used casually by servers and shopkeepers — don't take it personally; it's not flirting.

## Useful in a restaurant

- **¿Me trae el menú, por favor?** — Could you bring me the menu?
- **¿Qué me recomienda?** — What do you recommend?
- **Sin cilantro, por favor** — Without cilantro, please (cilantro shows up everywhere)
- **Está delicioso** — It's delicious
- **¿Tienen opciones vegetarianas?** — Do you have vegetarian options?

## Useful in a taxi / Uber

- **¿Puede ir más despacio, por favor?** — Could you go slower, please?
- **Aquí está bien, gracias** — Here is fine, thanks
- **¿Acepta tarjeta?** — Do you accept card?

## Pro tip

Paisas use **"usted"** even with friends and family — much more than other Spanish speakers. Don't worry about the formal/informal distinction; use *usted* and you'll always be fine.
