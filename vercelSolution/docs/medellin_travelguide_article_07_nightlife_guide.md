# Medellín nightlife guide

Medellín's nightlife is famous — and not just for the obvious reasons. Here's how to do it well.

## The neighborhoods

- **Parque Lleras (El Poblado)**
  - The tourist epicenter. Loud, crowded, packed with bars, salsa clubs, and street vendors selling arepas at 3 AM.
  - Trade-off: prices are higher and the scene is heavily tourist-driven.

- **Provenza**
  - Just a few blocks from Lleras but more upscale. Cocktail bars, rooftop terraces, better-looking crowds.

- **Carrera 70 (Laureles)**
  - Where locals actually go out. Salsa, reggaeton, fritanga restaurants, no cover. Much cheaper than Poblado.

- **Las Palmas (the hills)**
  - Big-club night out — Vintrash, La Octava, etc. Take an Uber up; you can't walk.

## Types of spots

- **Salsa clubs**: El Eslabón Prendido (Wed), Son Habana, Tibiri. Live bands and serious dancers — beginners welcome but humble.
- **Reggaeton clubs**: anything in Las Palmas after midnight.
- **Rooftop cocktails**: Ammazza Provenza, Envy Rooftop at The Charlee Hotel.
- **Live music / electronic**: Salón Amador for techno; Calle 9+1 for variety.

## Practical tips

- **The night starts late.** Bars fill up after 10 PM, clubs around midnight, and stay open until 3–4 AM.
- **Wednesday is the new Saturday.** Many places have their best nights mid-week.
- **Dress code**: most clubs require closed shoes (no flip-flops or athletic sandals).
- **Cover charges**: 20,000–40,000 COP at the bigger venues on weekends. Usually includes a drink.
- **Watch your drink at all times.** See [[medellin_travelguide_article_05_safety_tips_for_travelers]].
