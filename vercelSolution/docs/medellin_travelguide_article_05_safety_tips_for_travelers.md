# Safety tips for travelers in Medellín

Medellín is dramatically safer than it was 30 years ago, but it's still a big Latin American city. Use common sense and you'll be fine. The locals have a saying: **"no dar papaya"** — literally "don't give papaya," meaning don't make yourself an easy target.

## The basics

- **Don't flash valuables.** Phones, watches, jewelry — keep them out of sight on the street, especially while walking.
- **Use Uber or DiDi instead of street taxis.** Cheaper and you have a record of the trip.
- **Avoid empty streets at night.** Stick to busy areas — El Poblado, Laureles, Provenza are well-lit and active.
- **Carry a "decoy" amount of cash.** Around 50,000–100,000 COP. If something happens, hand it over without arguing.

## Specific things to know

- **Scopolamine ("burundanga")**: a sedative drug used to rob tourists, usually slipped into a drink or even sprayed on a piece of paper. Never accept open drinks from strangers. Be cautious about meeting people from dating apps for the first time at your hotel.
- **ATMs**: only use ATMs inside banks, supermarkets, or shopping malls. Avoid street ATMs at night.
- **Motorcycle theft**: snatch-and-grab of phones from people walking near the curb. Walk on the inside of the sidewalk.

## Areas to be careful in

- **Comuna 13**: completely safe during the day on the tourist route, but don't wander off it.
- **El Centro**: fine during the day with awareness, sketchy at night.
- **Parque Lleras at 3 AM**: where most tourist incidents happen. Leave before things get messy.

## Useful numbers

- **Police**: 123
- **Tourist police (English-speaking)**: +57 314 888 3838

**Bottom line:** Be aware, not paranoid. Most travelers have an amazing, incident-free trip.
