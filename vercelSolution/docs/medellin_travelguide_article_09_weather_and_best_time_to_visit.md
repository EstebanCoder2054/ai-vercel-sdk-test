# Weather and best time to visit Medellín

Medellín's nickname is **"La Ciudad de la Eterna Primavera"** — the City of Eternal Spring. At 1,500 meters above sea level and just 6 degrees from the equator, the temperature barely changes year-round.

## What to expect

- **Average daytime temperature**: 22–28°C (72–82°F)
- **Average nighttime temperature**: 16–18°C (61–64°F)
- **No real summer or winter** — just dry season vs. rainy season.

## Dry seasons (best for most travelers)

- **December – March**
- **July – August**

Sunny mornings, occasional afternoon clouds, low humidity. December has the **Alumbrados** Christmas light displays along the river — one of the city's most magical experiences.

## Rainy seasons

- **April – May**
- **September – November**

Don't let this scare you. Mornings are usually clear; rain comes in short heavy bursts in the afternoon and lasts an hour or two. Bring a light rain jacket. Trails and outdoor sites can get muddy.

## What to pack

- **Layers**: cool in the morning, warm at noon, cool again at night.
- **Light rain jacket** in any season.
- **Walking shoes** — the city has hills.
- **Sunscreen** — the equatorial sun is stronger than the temperature suggests.

## Special events worth timing for

- **Feria de las Flores** (early August): the city's biggest festival. Parades, flower displays, concerts.
- **Alumbrados Navideños** (December): millions of Christmas lights along the river.
- **Festival Internacional de Tango** (June): a quieter but beautiful event in honor of Carlos Gardel, who died in Medellín.
