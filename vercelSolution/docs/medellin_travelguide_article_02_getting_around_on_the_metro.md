# Getting around on the Medellín Metro

The Medellín Metro is the pride of the city — it's clean, cheap, safe, and the only metro system in Colombia. Here's what you need to know:

- **The Civica card**
  - Buy a rechargeable Civica card at any station for a small deposit. It works on the metro, metrocable (cable cars), tram, and most integrated buses.
  - Single tickets exist but the card saves time and a few hundred pesos per ride.

- **Lines to know**
  - **Line A** (north–south): the main artery, runs along the river. Most tourists use this.
  - **Line B** (east–west): connects to San Javier station, where you transfer for the Comuna 13 tour.
  - **Metrocable lines J, K, L, M, P**: cable cars that climb the surrounding hills. Line K up to Acevedo + Line L into Arví Park is a must-do day trip.

- **Hours**
  - Roughly 4:30 AM to 11:00 PM on weekdays, shorter on Sundays.

- **Etiquette**
  - Let people exit before boarding. Don't eat or drink inside. Locals take this seriously — the metro is considered a symbol of civic pride.

- **Safety**
  - Very safe during the day. After ~9 PM, prefer Uber for long trips, especially if you're alone.

**Pro tip:** The metrocable rides offer the best free views of the city. Bring a charged phone.
