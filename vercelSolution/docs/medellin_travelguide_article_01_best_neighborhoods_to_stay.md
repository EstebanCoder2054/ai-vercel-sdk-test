# Best neighborhoods to stay in Medellín

Choosing where to stay shapes your entire trip. Here are the top areas, with the trade-offs of each:

- **El Poblado**
  - The most popular area for tourists and digital nomads. Walkable, full of restaurants, cafés, and nightlife around Parque Lleras and Provenza.
  - Trade-off: most expensive, and feels less "local" than other areas.

- **Laureles**
  - Quieter, flatter, and more residential. Tree-lined streets, great food scene, and a strong local feel.
  - Trade-off: fewer hostels and English speakers; better for travelers who already speak some Spanish.

- **Envigado**
  - Technically a separate municipality but blends into the south of the city. Excellent food, friendly locals, very safe.
  - Trade-off: further from the main tourist spots; you'll Uber more.

- **El Centro (Downtown)**
  - Cheapest area, packed with history (Botero Plaza, Museo de Antioquia).
  - Trade-off: not recommended for sleeping — sketchy after dark.

- **Sabaneta**
  - Small-town vibe inside the metro area. Great parks and weekend markets.
  - Trade-off: limited nightlife, longer commute to El Poblado.

**Recommendation:** First-time visitors → El Poblado. Returning travelers or anyone staying 2+ weeks → Laureles.
