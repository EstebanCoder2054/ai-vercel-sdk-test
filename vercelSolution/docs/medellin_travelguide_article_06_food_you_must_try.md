# Paisa food you must try in Medellín

Antioquia's cuisine — known as *comida paisa* — is hearty, meat-heavy, and built for people who used to hike mountains all day. Here's what to eat and where.

## The classics

- **Bandeja paisa**
  - The national dish of the region. A massive plate with rice, red beans, ground beef, chicharrón (fried pork belly), chorizo, fried egg, fried plantain, avocado, and an arepa.
  - Try it at: **Mondongo's** (Provenza) or **Hacienda Junín** (El Centro).

- **Arepa paisa**
  - Thin, white-corn arepa, usually grilled. The local version is plainer than the Venezuelan one — meant as a side, not a sandwich.
  - Best with butter and salty *quesito* cheese.

- **Sancocho**
  - A hearty soup with chicken or beef, yuca, potato, plantain, and corn. Sunday lunch food.

- **Chicharrón**
  - Crispy fried pork belly. Eat it as part of a bandeja or on its own with arepa and lime.

- **Empanadas**
  - Small fried corn-flour pockets filled with potato and meat. Street food staple — 2,000–3,000 COP each.

## Drinks

- **Aguardiente Antioqueño**: the local anise-flavored sugar-cane spirit. Drink it as a shot, ice-cold.
- **Refajo**: beer + Colombiana (a red cream-soda). Surprisingly good at a barbecue.
- **Lulada / mango biche / guanábana juice**: incredible Colombian fruits you can't get elsewhere.

## Pro tips

- Lunch (almuerzo) is the main meal. Many restaurants offer a 3-course *menú del día* for 15,000–25,000 COP.
- Vegetarian options exist but are limited — Plato Fuerte (Laureles) and Verdeo (Poblado) are the go-tos.
