# Best specialty coffee shops in Medellín

Colombia is one of the world's great coffee-producing countries, and Medellín's specialty café scene has exploded in the last 10 years. Here's where to drink the good stuff:

## El Poblado / Provenza

- **Pergamino Café** — the most famous specialty café in the city. Comes from a family of coffee growers. Always busy.
- **Café Velvet** — minimalist, great pour-overs, strong reputation among baristas.
- **Hija Mía** — solid beans, beautiful interior, packed with remote workers on weekdays.

## Laureles

- **Rituales Café** — quiet, friendly, no-laptop vibe. Great for an actual conversation.
- **Café Revolución Origen** — direct trade with small farms, rotating single-origin menu.

## El Centro

- **Pergamino Centro** — same brand, different vibe. Less crowded than the Poblado branch.

## What to order

- **Tinto**: small black coffee, the everyday Colombian drink. Cheap and strong.
- **Pour-over (V60 or Chemex)**: how specialty cafés show off single-origin beans.
- **Cold brew**: most cafés have it; great in Medellín's warm afternoons.

## Pro tips

- Beans are the cheapest souvenir you can bring home. Most cafés ship internationally too.
- Ask for *café de origen* (single-origin) — that's where the magic is.
- Avoid Juan Valdez if you're a coffee snob; it's the Starbucks of Colombia.
