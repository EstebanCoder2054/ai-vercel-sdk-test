# Day trip to Guatapé and El Peñol

Guatapé is the #1 day trip from Medellín. Two hours away by car, it combines a giant climbable rock with the most colorful town in Colombia.

## What you'll do

- **Climb El Peñol (La Piedra)**
  - A 220-meter granite monolith with 740 zigzag stairs built up one side.
  - The view from the top — a maze of green islands and turquoise water — is the postcard shot of Colombia.
  - Entrance fee is around 25,000 COP. Bring water; it gets hot.

- **Walk through Guatapé town**
  - Every house has a hand-painted *zócalo* (a decorated panel at the base of the wall) depicting local life, family trades, or animals.
  - Plaza de los Zócalos and Calle del Recuerdo are the prettiest streets.

- **Boat tour on the reservoir**
  - 30,000–50,000 COP per person for a 60-minute tour past Pablo Escobar's bombed-out mansion (now a tourist site of its own).

## How to get there

- **Organized tour (~80,000–150,000 COP)**: easiest option. Hotel pickup, English guide, lunch included.
- **Public bus**: leaves from Terminal del Norte every hour. Around 18,000 COP one-way, takes 2.5 hours.
- **Private Uber/driver**: about 250,000 COP round-trip — splits well for groups of 4.

## Tips

- Leave early (7 AM) — the rock gets crowded by 11 AM.
- Wear sneakers; the stairs are steep.
- One day is enough, but staying overnight lets you enjoy the town after the day-trippers leave.
