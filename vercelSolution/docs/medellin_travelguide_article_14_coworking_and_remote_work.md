# Coworking and remote work in Medellín

Medellín has become one of the top remote-work hubs in Latin America. Fast fiber, perfect weather, low cost, and dozens of coworking options.

## Top coworking spaces

- **Selina (El Poblado)**
  - Hostel + coworking combo. Great if you're new in town and want to meet people fast.
  - Day passes around 50,000 COP.

- **WeWork Manila / WeWork 73**
  - Two locations in El Poblado. Most reliable Wi-Fi, full coffee bar, quiet meeting rooms.
  - Monthly passes around 1,200,000–1,800,000 COP.

- **Tinkko**
  - Multiple locations in Poblado, Laureles, and Envigado. Mid-priced and well-designed.

- **Atom House (Laureles)**
  - Smaller community, great for longer stays, lots of nomads.

- **La Casa Redonda (Envigado)**
  - Slightly out of the way, quieter, very friendly.

## Cafés that welcome laptops

- **Hija Mía** (Provenza) — laptop-friendly, fast Wi-Fi.
- **Botanika Café** (Laureles) — outdoor seating, plants everywhere.
- **Café Cliché** (Provenza) — French vibes, good for video calls in the corners.
- **Pergamino** is great for coffee but **not** great for working — it gets too packed.

## Internet speeds

- **Fiber in El Poblado / Laureles**: typically 200–600 Mbps in modern apartments
- **Mobile (4G/5G)**: 30–100 Mbps in the city
- **Power outages**: rare but happen. If you take a lot of calls, a UPS or a backup hotspot is worth it.

## Visa note

Colombia offers a **digital nomad visa** (Visa V) for remote workers earning a minimum monthly income (around 3x the Colombian minimum wage in USD). Initial duration up to 2 years. Apply via [migracioncolombia.gov.co](https://www.migracioncolombia.gov.co).

Most short-term visitors come in on the **90-day visa-free entry** stamp (extendable once for another 90 days).

## Community

- **Nomad List Medellín group** and the **Medellín Digital Nomads Facebook group** are where most events get posted.
- **Spanish + English language exchanges** happen weekly at several bars in Poblado.
