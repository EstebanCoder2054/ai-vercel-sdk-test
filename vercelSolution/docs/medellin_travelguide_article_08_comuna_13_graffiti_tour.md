# Comuna 13 graffiti tour

Once one of the most dangerous neighborhoods in the world, Comuna 13 has transformed into Medellín's most powerful tourist experience — a living open-air museum of street art, hip-hop, and resilience.

## Why it matters

In the late 1990s and early 2000s, Comuna 13 was the front line of conflict between guerrillas, paramilitaries, gangs, and the army. The 2002 military "Operación Orión" left dozens of civilians dead. The neighborhood's recovery is told through its murals.

## How to visit

- **Take a guided tour.** This is the one place where a guide makes a huge difference — the murals only make sense with the stories behind them. Tours run 3–4 hours and cost 60,000–100,000 COP per person.
- **Recommended tour companies**: Zippy Tour, Comuna 13 Real City Tours, and several local-led options that hire residents directly.
- **Don't go alone after dark.** The tourist route is safe in daylight, but the surrounding alleys are still rough.

## What you'll see and do

- **The outdoor electric escalators** — installed in 2011, the symbol of the transformation. The neighborhood is built on a steep hillside; the escalators connect six levels.
- **Murals by Chota, Perrograff, AKS, Yes, Kbala** — each artist's signature style.
- **Live hip-hop performances** from neighborhood groups like Casa Kolacho.
- **Mango biche con sal y limón** — green mango with salt and lime, the iconic street snack.

## What to bring

- Cash for snacks, art, and tipping your guide (10–20% is standard).
- Comfortable shoes — the route involves stairs and slopes.
- Sunscreen and a hat — the sun is direct.

## Etiquette

- Ask before photographing residents, especially kids.
- Tip street performers if you stop to watch.
- Don't reduce the neighborhood to its violent past — talk to your guide about what life is like there *now*.
