# Side trips beyond Medellín

Medellín is a fantastic base for exploring the rest of Antioquia and beyond. Here are the best multi-day side trips, sorted by travel time.

## Within 2–3 hours

- **Guatapé + El Peñol**
  - The classic day trip. See [[medellin_travelguide_article_03_day_trip_to_guatape]] for a full guide.

- **Jardín**
  - A tiny, postcard-perfect coffee town 3 hours south. Cobblestone streets, painted balconies, a beautiful plaza where everyone gathers at sunset.
  - Don't miss: the *Garrucha* cable car (a metal box on a wire), the Cueva del Esplendor waterfall hike, and the surrounding coffee farms.
  - Stay 1–2 nights.

- **Santa Fe de Antioquia**
  - Colonial town 1.5 hours from Medellín, much hotter than the city (30°C+). Famous for the *Puente de Occidente* — a 19th-century suspension bridge.

## Within 4–6 hours

- **Salento + Valle de Cocora (Eje Cafetero)**
  - The heart of Colombia's coffee region. Hike among the world's tallest palm trees in Valle de Cocora.
  - Closest airport is Pereira or Armenia (45-min flight from Medellín). Many travelers also go via Jardín → Salento by jeep, which is its own adventure.
  - Stay 2–3 nights minimum.

- **Cartagena (by plane)**
  - 1-hour flight to the Caribbean coast. Walled colonial city, beaches, completely different climate (hot and humid).
  - Stay 2–4 nights.

## For the adventurous

- **Capurganá and Sapzurro** (Pacific/Caribbean border)
  - No road access — fly to Acandí or take a boat. Untouched beaches, jungle, diving.

- **Caño Cristales** (Meta)
  - The "river of five colors." Best seen July–November. Fly via Bogotá or La Macarena.

- **Tatacoa Desert** (Huila)
  - One of the best stargazing spots in Colombia.

## Booking tips

- **Domestic flights**: Avianca, LATAM, and Wingo. Book 3–4 weeks ahead for the best prices.
- **Buses**: Cheap and comfortable. Terminal del Norte for trips north (e.g., to the coast), Terminal del Sur for south (e.g., Eje Cafetero).
- **Rent a car**: only worth it if you're confident with mountain roads — Antioquia's geography is no joke.
